  • ● March 2026 sales reach 285,854 units, growing 2.7 percent year-on-year, driven primarily by global SUV models
  • ● Kia is named “Best Manufacturer” at the TopGear.com EV Awards 2026 for its consistent development of electric vehicle line-up globally

 

Jakarta, 17 April 2026 – Kia Corporation begins 2026 with performance that reflects consistency amid the shifting landscape of the global automotive industry. From January to March, Kia recorded global sales of 779,169 units, marking the highest first-quarter performance in the company’s history, with a 0.8 percent increase compared to the same period last year.

This achievement comes amid increasingly complex market dynamics, where growth is no longer driven solely by volume expansion, but also by a brand’s ability to maintain product relevance across various segments and regions. In this context, Kia’s performance indicates that the market continues to respond positively to its product portfolio, even as consumer preferences begin shifting toward more integrated technology and higher efficiency.

Q1 2026 Performance Reflects Kia’s Consistency Amid Global Mobility Shifts

Globally, Kia’s performance in the first quarter of 2026 shows steady growth, with key contributions coming from markets outside Korea, recording 228,978 units sold in March, as well as growth in the Korean market, which reached 56,404 units, up 12.8 percent compared to the same period last year. In Europe, Kia also recorded a new monthly sales record, with a total of 58,750 units sold.

In the Korean market, the Sorento also led passenger vehicle sales with 10,870 units, followed by the Sportage with 5.540 units and the Carnival with 5.407 units. Demand for SUV models remains a key foundation of this performance. The Kia Sportage recorded global sales of 48,885 units in March, followed by the Seltos with 31,761 units and the Sorento with 21.285 units. These three models continue to play a consistent role in supporting sales volume, while reflecting strong market preference for the SUV segment.

On the other hand, electric vehicles are beginning to show more measurable contributions, particularly in the Korean market, with a total of 16,187 units sold in March, led by EV3, EV5, and PV5. This figure indicates that electrification is no longer in its early stage, but is entering a more commercially relevant phase, in line with product readiness and market acceptance.

These results are aligned with the strengthening of Kia’s global electric vehicle (EV) portfolio, which is developed to address an increasingly broad range of consumer needs. This line-up spans multiple vehicle segments, including models such as EV6, EV9, and PV5, reflecting Kia’s approach in delivering electric vehicles with distinct characteristics and functions tailored to different user requirements. Electrification is no longer positioned as a separate line, but as an integral part of overall product development.

In line with this, Kia has been named “Best Manufacturer” at the TopGear.com EV Awards 2026, which evaluates the overall electric vehicle line-up available in the UK market. This recognition highlights Kia’s consistent approach in delivering electric vehicles, not only in terms of performance, but also comfort, ease of use, and relevance to user needs across segments.

“The developments we see globally reflect increasingly dynamic consumer expectations, including in Indonesia. Kia consistently builds a direction that focuses not only on products, but also on how technology, desain, and exclusive experiences can be delivered in a way that is relevant to everyday life. Going forward, we aim to ensure that consumers in Indonesia can access the same standards and innovations that we deliver in global markets,” said Jong Sung Park, CEO of Kia Sales Indonesia.
