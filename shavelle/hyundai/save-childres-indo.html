Jakarta, 27 January 2026 – 29 tons of plastic waste have officially been 'removed' from Jakarta's environmental pollution chain through the Hyundai Continue initiative, a Creating Shared Value (CSV) activity focusing on social impact as part of Hyundai’s social responsibility as a member of the Indonesian community.

 

This figure is the result of a four-year commitment to the Circular Economy program initiated by Hyundai Motor Company and Save the Children Indonesia. This achievement was shared during the “Performance Sharing Ceremony: Hyundai Motor Circular Economy Project” held at the Hyundai N Brand Experience Center.

 

Based on data from the National Waste Management Information System (SIPSN) of the Ministry of Environment in 2025, as much as 68% of waste in Jakarta is still not managed optimally, with plastic being the second-largest contributor.

 

Responding to this issue, since 2022, the Circular Economy program has initiated the Cerdas Pilah Plastik (Smart Plastic Sorting) campaign to increase awareness and change public behavior regarding the importance of sorting plastic waste.

 

This program does not stop at education. A total of 100 waste-sorting dropbox units have been distributed across various strategic points in North Jakarta and South Jakarta. Interestingly, the collected waste is then recycled into high-value products such as t-shirts and dolls, proving that a circular economy is a tangible solution for the environment as well as a new economic opportunity.

 

“Through Hyundai Continue, Hyundai is committed to advancing sustainability initiatives that go beyond environmental preservation to help shape a better future for generations to come. Guided by our ‘Progress for Humanity’ vision, we define progress as tangible action that creates a positive impact on both people and the planet, including ensuring that children can grow and thrive in a safe and healthy environment,” saidKenny Lee, President Director of Hyundai Motors Indonesia.

 

“Our four-year collaboration with Save the Children Indonesia through this Circular Economy Program shows that this commitment becomes more meaningful when the government, communities, and the wider public work together. By successfully diverting nearly 29 tons of plastic waste from Jakarta’s environment, we hope this initiative can serve as a sustainable foundation that continues to be strengthened in collaboration with stakeholders going forward,” he added.

 

The Voice of Children as a Driver of Change

The uniqueness of this program lies in the active involvement of Child Campaigners from 20 schools and 4 RPTRA (Integrated Child-Friendly Public Spaces) in DKI Jakarta. They stand at the forefront, voicing the impact of plastic waste on the quality of life for future generations, ranging from health risks to the loss of safe play spaces.

 

“Every gram of plastic we sort today is a breath of fresh air for our children in the future. Through this program, we are not just cleaning the city, but building children’s sovereignty over their environment so that their right to grow in a healthy environment is not buried by plastic waste,” said Evie Woro Yulianti, Senior Manager of Inclusive Youth Empowerment & Urban Programming at Save the Children Indonesia.

 

During the event, children expressed their aspirations through a musical drama themed on the impact of plastic waste on children's rights, and shared stories through photo media documenting the initiatives they have undertaken in their respective schools and communities.

 

With a spirit of collaboration, education, and real action, the Circular Economy program has succeeded in transforming the face of waste management from a mere problem into an opportunity, proving that small steps from schools to communities are capable of creating a significant and sustainable impact.

 

The active involvement of children, supported by partnerships with the government, serves as a vital foundation in strengthening the program's impact. This synergy ensures that waste management efforts are not only sustainable but also aligned with children's rights to live and grow in a safe and healthy environment.
