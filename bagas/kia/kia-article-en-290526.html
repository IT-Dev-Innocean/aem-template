Hidden Risks of Using Fake Car Spare Parts for Your Engine

 

Often, fake or imitation spare parts are packaged in such a way that they look identical to the original. However, "visual similarity" never guarantees "performance similarity." ICE engines are very sensitive to precision; a difference of just one millimeter in a component can be fatal.

 

Here are some risks often encountered in workshops due to the use of non-standard parts:

 

1. Filtration Failure in Vital Components

Take the oil filter as an example. Genuine filters are designed to filter microscopic particles without obstructing the oil flow to the top of the engine. Fake filters often use filter paper that is too dense (obstructing flow) or too loose (letting dirt through). As a result, components like the camshaft and pistons can suffer scratches due to imperfect lubrication.

 

2. Low Material Durability

Components like imitation timing belts or v-belts may look strong, but they often cannot withstand the extreme heat inside the engine bay. If this belt snaps while the car is moving, the engine valves can collide with the pistons, resulting in total engine failure. The cost of replacing one genuine belt is much cheaper than performing a full engine overhaul.

 

3. Interference in Electrical and Combustion Systems

The use of imitation spark plugs or sensors can cause an incomplete combustion process. This triggers symptoms such as knocking (ngelitik), wasteful fuel consumption, and even damage to expensive components like the catalytic converter. Investing in genuine car spare parts ensures the synergy between electrical and mechanical systems remains maintained.

 

Direct Impact on Vehicle Safety and Warranty

 

Many people forget that using genuine parts is not just about keeping the engine from breaking down, but also about safety. Components like imitation brake pads or brake hoses have very low friction coefficients and heat resistance. In sudden braking situations, fake parts risk experiencing fading (brake failure), which endangers you and your family.
