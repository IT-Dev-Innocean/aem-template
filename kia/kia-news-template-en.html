• Kia continues the momentum from IIMS Jakarta 2026 by showcasing its flagship line-up while bringing interactive booth activations through the World Cup AI Photobooth and Hanbok Costume Experience.
  • Kia reinforces its strategic direction in Indonesia through dealer network expansion, global line-up development, and future-ready mobility solutions for Indonesian consumers.
  • The all-new Carens, The new Sonet, and Seltos return as representations of Kia’s line-up that combines global excellence with the lifestyle needs of Indonesian consumers.

 

Surabaya, 26 May 2026 – Kia continues the momentum built since IIMS Jakarta last February through its participation in IIMS Surabaya 2026 which is held in Grand City Convex, Surabaya. During today’s press conference, Kia reaffirmed its strategic direction in Indonesia through several key focuses, ranging from Kia’s 2026 vision through strengthening local manufacturing, sustainable mobility, and dealer network expansion across Indonesia, particularly in Surabaya.

At IIMS Surabaya, Kia also presents a more interactive booth experience themed around the World Cup AI Photobooth and Hanbok Costume Experience. Visitors can enjoy a more personal and playful photo experience while immersing themselves in the more expressive atmosphere of the Kia booth throughout IIMS Surabaya 2026.

Jong Sung Park, CEO of Kia Sales Indonesia, stated during his opening remarks, “Following the launch of The all-new Carens and The new Sonet at IIMS Jakarta, today we continue that momentum through Kia’s presence at IIMS Surabaya 2026, where we remain committed to delivering products and experiences that combine global innovation with consumer needs. From expanding our dealer network and improving service accessibility to introducing future-ready products,” he said.

Kia Continues Momentum at IIMS Surabaya 2026 with Flagship Line-Up

Kia’s presence at IIMS Surabaya 2026 also showcases various flagship models representing Kia’s product development direction for the Indonesian market. The all-new Carens, officially launched last February at IIMS Jakarta and becoming one of Kia’s main sales contributors during the exhibition, returns in Surabaya as a 6/7-seater MPV designed for the lifestyle needs of modern Indonesian families. Meanwhile, The new Sonet appears as a compact and stylish small A-SUV built to support increasingly dynamic urban mobility.

The East Java OTR prices for The all-new Carens are as follows:

  • Trendy : Rp309.900.000
  • Motion : Rp349.900.000
  • Signature : Rp389.900.000

Meanwhile, the East Java OTR prices for The new Sonet are as follows:

  • ● Trendy : Rp273.900.000
  • ● Motion : Rp298.900.000
  • ● Signature : Rp323.900.000

Kia also reintroduces the Seltos, which was first unveiled at IIMS Jakarta last February. Positioned in the compact B-SUV segment, Seltos previously earned the “SUV of the Year” award at the 2026 Times Drive Auto Summit & Awards and was also included in the “Top Pick for First-Time Car Buyers” by Newsweek Autos Awards 2024 globally. The latest generation Seltos comes with a bolder and more modern design character, advanced features, and a driving experience tailored for both daily mobility and long-distance journeys.

Kia also reaffirmed the strategic pillars that serve as the foundation for developing its line-up for the Indonesian market, including delivering products with competitive value and superior line-ups relevant to consumer needs, designing vehicles with World Class Design and premium in-car experiences, as well as building progressive digital engagement while continuing to prioritize an ergonomic driving experience that is more seamless and easier to use in daily activities.

Harry Yanto, VP Operations of Kia Sales Indonesia, emphasized, “The line-up we are presenting at IIMS Surabaya 2026 reflects how Kia continues to deliver products that are relevant to the needs and lifestyles of Indonesian consumers today. From modern family MPVs and compact urban SUVs to compact B-SUVs with a more dynamic character. Every Kia line-up is developed with competitive value, world class design, and progressive digital engagement so consumers can enjoy a more seamless experience that supports their everyday needs,” he said.

Through its participation at IIMS Surabaya 2026, Kia aims to further strengthen its connection with Indonesian consumers while showcasing the brand’s increasingly progressive direction for the future. With a more complete line-up, expanding dealer network, introduction of new products for the Indonesian market, as well as support through Kia Total Care services and other aftersales programs, Kia remains optimistic about growing alongside the development of Indonesia’s automotive industry, particularly in East Java as one of the country’s largest automotive markets.
