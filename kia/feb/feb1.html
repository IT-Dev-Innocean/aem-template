• ● Kia views IIMS 2026 as a strategic dialogue platform to understand market dynamics and Indonesian automotive consumer behavior.
  • ● Kia’s inaugural debut in Indonesia through IIMS 2026 becomes a key momentum to introduce a brand identity and approach that are more relevant to the local market.
  • ● Through booth experiences and direct interaction, Kia aims to build closer connections while opening long-term relationships with Indonesian consumers.

 

Jakarta, 29 January 2026 – In recent years, Indonesian consumers have shown significant changes in how they choose vehicles. Purchase decisions are no longer driven solely by brand or technical specifications, but also by experiences, values offered, and emotional connections with a brand. This condition reflects the growing maturity of the national automotive market, which is becoming increasingly rational and aware of its needs.

Responding to these dynamics, Kia is preparing its inaugural debut in Indonesia through its participation in the Indonesia International Motor Show (IIMS) 2026 as an initial step to build a closer understanding of the market. This presence marks an important moment for Kia to introduce its brand identity and approach directly to the Indonesian public.

“Entering the Indonesian market is a strategic step for Kia. Kia’s presence at IIMS 2026 is not merely participation in an automotive exhibition, but also reflects Kia’s understanding of the evolving dynamics and character of the Indonesian automotive market. Through analysis of consumer preferences, mobility trends, and the need for vehicles that are relevant to local conditions, Kia is preparing various initiatives and products designed to meet the expectations of the Indonesian market, while also communicating Kia’s new values and identity in a more relevant manner,” said Bayu Riyanto, Managing Director of Kia Sales Indonesia.

An Increasingly Diverse Automotive Market, More Selective Consumers

Today, the Indonesian automotive market is no longer moving in a single direction. Amid the growing number of vehicle choices and continuously evolving technologies, consumers are increasingly aware that choosing a car is not merely about brand or specifications, but about how well it fits their needs and lifestyle.

This perspective makes the vehicle selection process more personal. Consumers are beginning to consider how a vehicle is used on a daily basis, the level of comfort it offers, and the impressions they feel when interacting with a brand. Experiences, both before and behind the wheel, are becoming an important part of decision-making.

For Kia, this shift indicates that its presence in the Indonesian market needs to be built in a closer and more relevant way. Not only by introducing products, but also by understanding how Indonesian consumers perceive mobility in their everyday lives. From this point, Kia’s approach to the Indonesian market begins to take shape.

IIMS 2026 as a Space for Dialogue, Not Merely an Exhibition

For Kia, IIMS represents an important momentum to meet directly with Indonesian consumers and open broader dialogue about needs, expectations, and the market’s perspective on mobility. Kia will present various surprises at IIMS 2026 that reflect Kia’s new identity as a global brand with a more modern, relevant, and customer experience–oriented approach. One of the main highlights is Kia’s plan to officially introduce its latest product, which has been adapted to the needs and characteristics of the Indonesian automotive market.

Through its presence at IIMS 2026, Kia aims to directly observe and experience how Indonesian consumers respond to the design, technology, and overall experience presented. Interactions across the exhibition area, spontaneous reactions from visitors at the booth, and organic conversations that take place on-site all form part of Kia’s effort to better understand the dynamics of the Indonesian market in an authentic and meaningful way. As part of this experience, Kia will introduce a number of Korean culture–inspired touches within the booth area, serving as complementary elements that enrich the visitor experience while reflecting the character of the Kia brand.

“IIMS 2026 marks an important moment for us to engage more closely with Indonesian consumers. Through the various experiences and interactions we have prepared at the exhibition area, including experiential elements that reflect Kia’s character and brand identity, we aim to listen directly to public feedback while introducing Kia as a brand that is relevant to the needs and lifestyle of Indonesian society. This serves as an initial step in building a long-term relationship with the Indonesian market,” concluded Bayu Riyanto.
