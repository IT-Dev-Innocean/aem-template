• ● Kia presented two exhibitions themed ‘Resonance of Opposites’ at Milan Design Week 2026 as a representation of the evolution of its ‘Opposites United’ design philosophy
  • ● ‘Journey of Reflection’ highlighted Kia’s creative process and design perspectives through a series of interactive artistic installations
  • ● ‘Journey of Projection’ showcased six Kia concept vehicles representing the brand’s future mobility design and innovation direction

 

MILAN/SEOUL, 18 May 2026 – Kia Corporation returned to Milan Design Week 2026 with two exhibitions themed ‘Resonance of Opposites’. Guided by its ‘Opposites United’ design philosophy, Kia presented an experience that illustrates how reflection, cultural perspectives, and creative exploration are translated into product design and innovation. The exhibitions took place from April 20 to 26, 2026 at Museo della Permanente and Salone dei Tessuti in Milan, Italy.

Since 2023, Kia has consistently used Milan Design Week as a platform to expand creative dialogue and showcase the evolution of its design philosophy to a global audience. This year, Kia introduced a more reflective approach by highlighting the thought processes behind its designers’ work, as well as how those ideas evolve into tangible forms and experiences through product design.

Karim Habib, Executive Vice President and Head of Kia Global Design stated, “Our presence in Milan this year marks an important moment for Kia Design to demonstrate how creative reflection and design expression can exist side by side. ‘Resonance of Opposites’ is more than an exhibition and represents part of Kia’s ongoing journey to evolve and deliver more meaningful innovation,” he said.

Journey of Reflection: Exploring Kia’s Design Perspectives

Through ‘Journey of Reflection’ at Museo della Permanente, Kia invited visitors to take a closer look at the creative process behind Kia Design. The exhibition was built around three key cultural characteristics that illustrate how Kia designers interpret perspectives, emotions, and cultural shifts throughout the design process.

‘Cultural Vanguard’ featured a dynamic light installation with continuously moving and shifting colors. The installation symbolized how different perspectives can converge to shape new cultures and ideas.

‘Creative Risk-Takers' introduced an interactive space with shapes and contours that changed according to visitors’ movements. The installation represented Kia’s boldness in exploring new ideas and encouraging a more experimental and unconventional design approach.

‘Relentless Innovators’ presented a calm atmosphere through slowly shifting light and color compositions. The installation reflected Kia’s consistency in developing innovation through an ongoing and evolving process over time.

In the evening, the exhibition also featured talk sessions and artist performances involving designers, artists, and creative figures from various disciplines, sharing perspectives and interpretations related to the exhibition themes.

Journey of Projection: Kia’s Futuristic Design Direction

Through ‘Journey of Projection’ at Salone dei Tessuti, Kia demonstrated how its design philosophy is translated into concept vehicles and the future direction of mobility design.

The exhibition featured six concept vehicles, EV2 (Begin), EV3 (Explore), EV4 (Create), EV5 (Connect), EV9 (Embrace), and Kia Vision Meta Turismo (Imagine). Each vehicle represented a different stage in Kia’s evolving design strategy.

“We want visitors to see how the exploration process behind Kia designers’ ideas evolves into tangible forms through our concept vehicle lineup. We also want to demonstrate how Kia’s design philosophy comes to life in every creative decision we make,” added Karim Habib.

Overall, this EV concept lineup reflects Kia’s design exploration centered around user freedom, progressive form interpretation, and mobility experiences tailored for future generations. The consistency of this design philosophy is also reflected through the various global awards received by Kia.

The Kia EV9, Kia’s flagship electric SUV, received recognition from three major global design awards in 2024, including the Red Dot Design Award, iF Design Award, and IDEA (International Design Excellence Awards), further reinforcing Kia’s position in delivering electric vehicle design innovation in the electrification era.

In addition, the Kia EV3 received the Red Dot ‘Best of the Best’ Award 2025 in the Automobiles and Vehicles category. This achievement follows the success of the Kia EV9 in 2024 and the EV6 in 2022, while also reflecting the consistency of Kia’s ‘Opposites United’ design philosophy across its EV lineup.

This design philosophy is also aligned with Kia’s commitment in Indonesia to deliver World Class Design for consumers. Through the combination of modern design, technology, and user-oriented cabin comfort, Kia continues to deliver a premium driving experience in every journey.

Kia’s Evolution at Milan Design Week

The 2026 exhibition marked Kia’s fourth consecutive participation at Milan Design Week, continuing its exploration of the ‘Opposites United’ design philosophy through collaborations with artists and creators from around the world.

In 2023, Kia made its Milan Design Week debut through an exhibition highlighting the core foundation of Kia design, inspired by contrasts between nature and human life. Various artistic installations spread across seven exhibition areas combined video, sound, and sculptural elements to showcase Kia’s vision for a new era of sustainable mobility design.

In 2024, Kia presented an exhibition titled ‘Intersections Beyond Boundaries’, exploring freedom of expression while expanding creative perspectives. Through a series of art installations, Kia invited visitors to discover how new ideas can emerge by reinterpreting conventional boundaries through a more progressive and bold design approach.

In 2025, Kia introduced two major themes simultaneously. ‘Eclipse of Perceptions’ featured conceptual works by international artists such as Philippe Parreno and A.A. Murakami, encouraging visitors to engage directly with contemporary creativity. Meanwhile, ‘Transcend Journey’ showcased Kia’s latest PBV and EV lineup. The exhibition was combined with artistic works demonstrating how mobility can enrich user experiences through the fusion of technological innovation and an exploratory lifestyle.
