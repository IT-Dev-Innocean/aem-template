Myth: Big Cars Are Always Fuel-Hungry

 

For years, there has been a common assumption that the bigger a car’s dimensions are, the more fuel it will consume. In the past, this may have been true because large cars relied on very big-displacement engines and extremely heavy steel bodies.

 

However, modern automotive engineering has proven that myth wrong. Car manufacturers now use high-strength steel body materials that are much lighter yet still strong to ensure safety. A lighter vehicle load automatically reduces the engine’s workload.

 

In addition, today’s combustion systems are fully computer-managed to ensure precise fuel injection. This means you can get a very roomy cabin without having to compromise on daily efficiency.

 

Key Criteria for a Fuel-Efficient and Capable Family Car

 

If you’re researching a new family vehicle, don’t focus only on fuel-consumption numbers on paper. Understand the technology behind them.

 

Below are the main technologies that can make a family vehicle significantly more fuel-efficient:

 

1. Modern Engine Technology

Efficiency starts from the vehicle’s heart. Look for cars that use the latest-generation engines with technology such as Dual Continuously Variable Valve Timing (Dual CVVT) or direct injection systems (Gasoline Direct Injection). These technologies manage valve timing so air and fuel mix optimally, resulting in cleaner and more efficient combustion.

 

2. Smart Transmission System

The transmission transfers power from the engine to the wheels. Smart transmissions, such as Intelligent Variable Transmission (IVT), are designed to minimize harsh gear changes. They help keep engine speed (RPM) at the most efficient point across various steady speeds—so fuel usage becomes much more economical.

 

3. Electrification Options (Hybrid) or Modern Diesel Engines

For families with high mobility needs, considering electrification (Hybrid) technology or a Common Rail Direct Injection (CRDi) diesel engine can be a smart financial move. A hybrid system uses an electric motor to support initial acceleration, helping cut fuel use in stop-and-go traffic. Meanwhile, modern diesel engines are known for strong low-end torque that is highly efficient for carrying heavier loads.

Smart Tips to Save Fuel (BBM) During Family Holidays

 

Even if your car is equipped with the most advanced engine technology, driving habits and trip preparation still play a crucial role. Terapkan tips praktis ini agar liburan Anda semakin hemat:

 

  • Maintain proper tire pressure: Under-inflated tires increase the contact patch with the road, forcing the engine to work harder and consume more fuel.
  • Be smart about luggage placement: Avoid loading too many items on a roof box unless it’s truly necessary. It disrupts the vehicle’s aerodynamics and makes the car fight stronger wind resistance.
  • Drive smoothly: Sudden acceleration (stomping on the gas abruptly) is the biggest enemy of efficiency. Keep your speed steady and use the vehicle’s momentum.
